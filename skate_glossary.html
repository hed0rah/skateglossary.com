TECHNICAL NOMENCLATURE & FUNDAMENTALS [-]
Frontside vs Backside
Direction of rotation or approach to obstacles
For spins/rotations:
• Frontside (FS) = Chest/front leads the rotation, you see where you're going
• Backside (BS) = Back leads, you rotate blind initially
• Regular stance: FS = counterclockwise (from above), BS = clockwise
• Goofy stance: FS = clockwise, BS = counterclockwise

For grinds/slides:
• Frontside = Approach with obstacle in front of you (facing it)
• Backside = Approach with obstacle behind you (back to it)
This is THE fundamental concept in skateboarding - everything references FS or BS

Regular vs Goofy
Your natural stance - which foot goes forward
• Regular = Left foot forward, push with right
• Goofy = Right foot forward, push with left
Neither is "correct" - just like being left or right handed

Frontfoot vs Backfoot
Specifies which foot executes flip or rotation
Example: Frontfoot casper vs backfoot casper - different foot initiates the trick, completely changes the mechanics

Pinch
Pressing/pinching truck against ledge or rail while grinding
The point where the truck/wheel meets the deck. Example: "Das pinch" on frontside crooked grind - you're pinching your way through using one wheel and part of the truck. Helps lock in grinds.

Stance Terminology Summary
The four ways to roll on a skateboard
1. Regular/Goofy - Your normal stance, rolling forward
2. Switch - Opposite stance, rolling forward
3. Fakie - Normal stance, rolling backward
4. Nollie - Normal stance, rolling forward, but popping from nose
Every trick can technically be done in all four of these stances

Front foot pushes, back foot stays on board (opposite of normal)

Drop In
Starting on transition by dropping from the coping into the ramp
Place tail on coping → Lean forward → Weight commits → Roll in
One of the scariest tricks for beginners but essential for transition skating. The key is committing your weight forward - hesitation = eating shit.
Fundamental transition skill that separates flatground from ramp skating. First major hurdle for new skaters learning vert/bowls.

Ollie
Jumping with the board by popping the tail
Back foot pops tail → Front foot slides up → Level board → Land
Invented by Alan "Ollie" Gelfand in 1978. Revolutionary trick that changed skateboarding forever - made street skating possible. Every trick builds on this.
THE fundamental skateboard trick. If you can't ollie, you can't really skate street.

Pop Shove-It
aka: Pop Shuv, Shuvit
Board rotates 180° horizontally while you jump
Pop → Scoop back foot → Board spins 180° → Catch and land
Foundation trick - teaches board control and rotation. The "pop" is key - this is different from the old school shove-it where you just scoop without popping. Backside pop shuv is standard, frontside is less common.

Obstacle Types & Terrain
Common skatepark and street obstacles
Street:
• Ledge - Flat grindable surface
• Out Ledge - Ledge at top of stairs/drop, requires "out" motion to land below
• Rail - Round or square metal bar
• Hubba - Ledge or rail down stairs
• Gap - Space to ollie over
• Euro Gap - Gap with raised bank on landing side
• Manual Pad - Low flat obstacle for manuals

Transition:
• Quarter Pipe - Single curved wall
• Half Pipe - Two facing quarters
• Vert Ramp - Half pipe with vertical section
• Bowl - Pool-shaped concrete
• Mini Ramp - Smaller half pipe (usually 4-6ft)
• Bank - Angled flat surface
• Hip - Two quarters meeting at angle
• Spine - Two quarters back-to-back
• Pyramid - Four-sided obstacle, can be skated from all angles
• Volcano - Conical ramp with all-around approach
• Coping - Metal/PVC edge on transition lip

Bowl-Specific:
• Deep End - Deeper side of bowl
• Shallow End - Shallower side of bowl
• Kidney Bowl - Asymmetrical kidney-shaped bowl
• Clover Bowl - Multiple bowls connected in clover pattern
• Pool Block - Raised platform/obstacle in bowl
• Tile - Smooth ceramic/tile surface (often slippery)
• Death Box - Square/rectangular section in bowl corner
• Channel - Gap/channel between bowl sections
• Extension - Extended vert section above coping

Mall Grab
Carrying your board by the trucks instead of proper grip
Holding board with grip tape against your palm, trucks in hand
Universal sign of a non-skater. Board should be carried by the nose/tail or under your arm with wheels against your body. Mall grab gets your hand dirty and looks ridiculous.
Term emerged in the 1990s from posers at shopping malls who carried boards but didn't skate.

Called "credit carding" because it snaps the board like breaking a credit card. Usually means your board is done.

Trucks too loose OR wheels too big → Hard turn → Wheel hits deck → You eat shit
Fixed by tightening trucks, using riser pads, or getting smaller wheels. Beginners often set trucks too loose.
